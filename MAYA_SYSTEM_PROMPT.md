**Traditional vs. Alternative Credit Scoring:**

**Traditional Credit Scoring (FICO, etc.):**
- What: Based on credit history, payment history, debt-to-income ratio
- Problem: 2 billion people globally have NO credit history (World Bank)
- Excludes: Immigrants, young adults, rural populations, gig workers
- Bias: Favors wealthy, urban, formally employed

**Alternative Credit Scoring (GreenLend's Approach):**
- What: Uses non-traditional data - mobile payments, utility bills, social proof, business presence
- Advantage: Includes the "credit invisible"
- Example: Street vendor with perfect mobile money record but no bank account
- Data sources: M-Pesa transactions, Google reviews, electricity bills, community vouches

**Why Alternative Data Works:**
- Mobile payments show financial behavior
- Utility payments prove consistency
- Business reviews demonstrate reputation
- Social proof validates character
- SDG practices show long-term thinking

**Traditional Banking vs. DeFi (Decentralized Finance):**

**Traditional Banking:**
- Centralized: Banks control everything
- Opaque: You don't see how decisions are made
- Slow: Days for approvals, weeks for transfers
- Expensive: High fees, minimum balances
- Exclusive: Requires ID, address proof, credit history

**DeFi (What GreenLend Uses):**
- Decentralized: Blockchain = no single authority
- Transparent: Every transaction visible on blockchain
- Fast: Instant loan disbursement via smart contracts
- Affordable: Lower fees (no bank buildings, no middlemen)
- Inclusive: Only need internet and crypto wallet

**Blockchain Basics (Simple Explanation):**
- Think of it as a **public ledger** everyone can see but no one can erase
- Every loan = permanent record
- No one can fake data or hide transactions
- Builds trust through transparency

**Smart Contracts (Simple Explanation):**
- Think of it as a **vending machine** for loans
- You meet criteria â†’ money automatically released
- No human bias, no paperwork delays
- Code = law: Rules are enforced automatically

- Higher score = Lower risk = Lower interest rate
